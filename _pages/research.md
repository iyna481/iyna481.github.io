  <p class="eyebrow">Research overview</p>
  <h1>Cloud microphysics and super-droplet modeling</h1>
  <p>My research focuses on cloud microphysics, aerosol-cloud interactions, turbulence, and atmospheric modeling.</p>
</div>

At the University of Hyogo, I work on super-droplet modeling of cloud microphysical processes under the supervision of Prof. Shin-ichiro Shima.

## Research topics

<div class="research-grid">
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Cloud chamber simulations</h3>
    <p>I study cloud microphysical processes using numerical simulations of laboratory cloud chamber environments, including convection and expansion chamber configurations.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>LES-SDM modeling</h3>
    <p>I use large-eddy simulation and the super-droplet method to investigate aerosol-dependent droplet activation, condensational growth, ice growth, and cloud microphysical variability.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Marine stratocumulus clouds</h3>
    <p>I analyze observational data from marine stratocumulus cloud campaigns to understand entrainment, mixing, and vertical variations of cloud microphysical properties.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Aerosol-cloud interactions</h3>
    <p>I am interested in how aerosol number concentration, composition, and turbulent transport influence cloud structure, precipitation processes, and cloud optical properties.</p>
  </div>
</div>
